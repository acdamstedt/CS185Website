About Screw It!

Screw It! is a mobile app that serves as a central hub for home maintenance help, and makes home maintenance decision-making through DIY (Do It Yourself) vs. professional services comparisons easy. The name "Screw It!" brings down the pressure of doing home maintenance and embodies the nature of doing home maintenance yourself.

Our Design Process

GR1: Needfinding

Our needfinding phase where we interviewed people to determine personal needs and thoughts regarding our problem domain.

GR2: POVs, HMWs, Brainstorming & Experience Prototypes

From our needfinding interviews, we deduced POVs and "How Might We?" questions to look deeper into and narrow our scope. With these, we were able to determine solutions and tested specific solutions using simple prototypes.

GR3: Website

This website showcases our project, process, and the team behind Screw It!

Explore Site

GR4: Concept Video

We narrowed on a specific solution and decided on the catchy name, "Screw It!". We did market research and discussed our values with this to ensure our solution is efficient and inclusive for users. Our concept video portrays our general solution and value.

Google Slides Slides (PDF) Concept Video (Youtube) Concept Video (.mp4)

GR5: Sketching, Low-Fi Prototype & User Testing

Our Low-fi Prototype of Screw It, conducted on paper. We sketched out task flows onto paper based on complexity and had potential users test it to illuminate any issues concerning our initial UI design.

Google Slides Slides (PDF) Sketching Report Sketching Report (PDF)

GR6: Medium-Fi Figma Prototype

Our Medium-Fi Prototype, created with Figma. We implemented our design with hard-coded elements. However, it demonstrates our three task flows with clear colorful UI and transitions.

Prototype (Figma) Google Slides Slides (PDF)

GR7: Heuristic Evaluation

Members of different teams evaluated our Medium-Fi Prototype using Nielsen's Heuristics and ranked by severity. This allowed us to see what violations were made and needed to be changed and improved with our prototype.

Our Hi-Fi Prototype, created with Figma. Some elements are still hard-coded, but it still demonstrates our task flows and changes based on our given Heuristic Evaluations.
